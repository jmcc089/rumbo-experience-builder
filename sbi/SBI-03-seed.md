# SBI-03 · Seed dataset

> **Model:** Sonnet 4.6, low effort (generation can dispatch sub-agents).
> **Depends on:** SBI-02 (schema must exist first — generate against the exact schema).
> **Objective:** Produce a coherent, credible seed dataset anchored to REAL El Salvador locations, conforming exactly to the SBI-02 schema, and load it into Neon.

---

## Principle
The dataset is generated by the LLM against the fixed schema, then loaded. It must be **coherent and credible**, anchored to real El Salvador geography (the differentiator). The transfer matrix in particular must respect real-world geographic truth — the human will review it, so keep the numbers realistic.

## What to produce

1. **Zones (~9–10):** the ones listed in SBI-02. Real places, correct region labels.

2. **Transfer matrix:** average point-to-point transfer minutes between every zone pair. Must be geographically plausible for El Salvador (a small country — most transfers 30 min to ~4 h). Examples of real relationships to respect: La Libertad↔Santa Ana are on opposite sides and cannot share a morning; San Salvador is central and close to most zones; Ataco/Ruta de las Flores is near Santa Ana; eastern zones (Jiquilisco, Alegría) are farther from the west. **Flag this table clearly in output** so the human can validate it.

3. **Providers (aim for density, not just variety):** enough providers that there are **multiple options per category per zone** — this redundancy is required so repair (SBI-13) has replacements when a piece falls through. Mix `formal` and `informal` with realistic `reliability_score` and `base_popularity`:
   - Popular/formal flagship providers (e.g. the star volcano tour) → high `base_popularity` (0.7–0.9), high reliability.
   - Informal/niche providers → lower popularity (0.1–0.3), lower reliability (more likely to not respond / to be disrupted).

4. **Experiences:** anchored to real ES activities — Santa Ana (Ilamatepec) volcano hike, Coatepeque lake, Ruta de las Flores coffee tours + villages, El Tunco/El Zonte surf, Suchitoto colonial town + Suchitlán lake, Joya de Cerén (UNESCO), highland coffee in Ataco/Alegría, Tazumal archaeology, Jiquilisco mangroves. Fill all fields: category, duration_min, open_days, open_from/to, net_price, capacity_per_slot, and `dependency` where it applies (sunrise volcano hikes → `sunrise_only`; certain coastal activities → `tide_dependent`).

5. **Lodging:** at least one option per tier (`budget`/`comfort`/`premium`) in the zones where travelers overnight, with realistic per-night net prices and capacity.

6. **Provider personalization answers:** 2–4 lines per provider across the fixed dimensions (special_occasions, dietary_options, privacy_options, extras_on_request). Keep them comparable across providers.

## Format
- Format is the build's choice (SQL inserts, or JSON/TS loaded by a script). Recommended: a TypeScript/JSON seed under `project/src/seed/` plus a `seed.ts` loader that inserts into Neon. Choose whatever integrates cleanest with the SBI-01 setup.

## Verification criteria
- All seed records conform to the SBI-02 schema (types, FKs, enums).
- Loading the seed into Neon succeeds with no constraint violations.
- For at least 3 categories, there are ≥2 providers in the same zone (redundancy check for repair).
- Every experience with a sunrise/tide dependency has the `dependency` field set.
- The transfer matrix covers every zone pair and is clearly flagged for human review.

## Do NOT
- Do NOT invent fantasy places — anchor to real El Salvador locations.
- Do NOT generate `Provider_017`-style soulless placeholder names — give credible names.
- Do NOT store any marked-up/client prices in the seed — seed holds NET prices only.
